## Key Points

- General introduction 
	- Leasehold estate
		- a property right binds future owners
	- Licence 
		- a personal right (only enforceable against the person who gave it)
	- Leaseholders have stronger statutory protection (especially under housing law)
	- **a valid lease requires**
		- <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">exclusive possession</mark>
		- for a terms certain
			- Must have a fixed start and end date [[LPA 1925]] s205(1)(xxvii)
				- This can be a fixed term (6 months, 1 year)
				- Periodic Term (renews automatically (weekly monthly yearly))
		- no exception applies
			- A lease fails (becomes a licence) if
				- no intentions to create legal relations [[Booked v Palmer]]
				- Service Occupancy (occupancy required for employment duties) [[Carroll v Manek]], [[Ludgate House v Ricketts]], 
- Leasehold Estates 
	- Exclusive Possession 
		- Right to keep out strangers and even landlord except limited entry rights for inspection or repair [[Lord Templeman in Street]]
	- Single Occupancy cases
		- lease exists if there is no genuine terms removing exclusive possession
		- terms that destroy exclusivity 
			- right for landlord to provide cleaning [[Marchant v Charters]]
			- mobility clause (landlord can move tenant) [[Dresden Estates v Collinson]]
			- Required to vacate at set time [[Aslan v Murphy]]
				- **IF any of these things happen this is not longer a lease but a licence**
		- Limited rights to enter and repair are compatible with a lease 
		- Pretence 
			- is a term with an air of total unreality its there but it would never happen [[Antoniades v Villiers]] [[Global 100 v Laleva]]
	- Multiple Occupancy Cases 
		- Shared spaces 
			- hard to show exclusive possession 
			- there are 2 options 
				- Joint Tenancy (one collective right) all occupiers together share one lease
				- the things they share 
					- possession 
						- each tenant has the same right to possess the whole property 
						- no ones has a specific room they share control of all of it 
					- Interest 
						- Each tenant has the same type of duration of interest 
						- all hold the same kind of lease (e.g. 1-year-term)
					- Title 
						- Each tenant's right comes from the same document of transaction 
						- everyone signs the same agreement 
					- Time
						- All tenants interests start at the same time 
						- no staggered move-ins ot later additions
				- Separate leases for rooms + Licence for sharing parts [[Camelot v Khoo]]
- Obligations of Landlord and Tenant
	- Repair and maintenance [[Landlord and Tenant Act 1985]] s11
		- applies to residential leases more then 7 years 
	- Landlord must 
		- Keep structure and exterior in repair (drains, pipes)
		- keep installations for water, gas, electricity, sanitation in working order
		- keep installations for heating and hot water in working order
	- Tenants may repair and deduct cost if landlord fails after notice
	- Cases and limitations 
		- [[Post office v Aquarius]] disrepair is something gets worst from previous condition 
		- [[Quick v Taff-Ely BC]] no liability for inherent defects 
		- [[Lee v Leeds City Council]] no general ECHR duty to keep in good condition 
- The homes (Fitness for Human Habitation) Act 2018
	- inserts s9A-C into the [[LTA 1985]]
	- applies only to leases 7 years and more 
	- implies a covenant that the property is fit for human habitation at start and throughout
	- tenants can sue directly for damages or injunctions
	- **Definition of Fitness s10 [[LTA 1985]]**
		- a house is unfit if "so defective that it is not reasonably suitable for occupation"
		- Factors include 
			- repairs 
			- damp
			- lighting 
			- ventilation
			- water
			- sanitation
			- food facilities
			- any HHSRS hazards
		- Exemptions 
			- Tenant's misuse
			- natural disasters 
			- tenant's own possession
			- lack of third party consent
- Tenants rights to stay in property
	- security of tenure and assured short hold tenancies 
		- Private landlords can evict without fault using [[Housing Act 1988]] s2
		- Notice 
			- written 2 months min
