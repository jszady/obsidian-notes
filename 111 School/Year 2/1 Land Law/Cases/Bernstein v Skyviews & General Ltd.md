# Facts

- A company flew over Mr. Bernstein’s home and took aerial photographs. He sued for trespass into his airspace.
## Issue

- Does a landowner’s right to land extend infinitely upwards into the sky?
## Decision

- No – ownership extends only to a _reasonable height_ necessary for ordinary use and enjoyment of the land.
## Reasoning

- The maxim “up to the heavens” no longer applies in modern society.
- Landowners have rights in airspace only as far as reasonably necessary.

## Ratio

- Landowners’ airspace rights are limited to the height required for ordinary use and enjoyment.
#### Exam use 

- Defines **vertical limits** of ownership.
