## Key Points
- Overview of the CJEU
	- Legal Basis: Article 19(1) TEU
		- The Court of Justice of the European Union shall ensure that the law is observed in the interpretation and application of the Treaties 
	- Institutions under the CJEU umbrella:
		- Courts of Justice (CJ)
		- General Court (gc)
		- Specialized courts (only where created by Parliament and Council)
	- Main Focus 
		- To ensure interpretation and application of EU law across all member states 
- Structure of the CJEU
	- The Court of Justice 
		- 27 Judges (1 per member state) 
		- 11 advocates Generals (gives impartial opinions before judgment)
		- Chambers
			- Plenary(27), Grand Chamber(13) Smaller chamber (3 or 5 judges)
		- Main roles 
			- decides actions brought by the MS, EU institutions, or individuals 
			- Gives Preliminary Ruling (Art 267 TFEU)
			- Reviews legality of EU acts (Art 263 TFEU)
	- The General Court 
		- 56 Judges (2 per member state)
		- no advocate general 
		- 6-year renewable terms 
		- handles 
			- Most direct actions 
			- some appeals from specialised courts 
	- Specialised Courts 
		- Created by Parliament + court for specific Disputes 
		- Example: EU Cilivl Service Tribunal 
- Direct Actions â€“ Article 263 TFEU
	- **Purpose:**  
		- Allows the CJEU to **review the legality of EU acts** and **annul them** if unlawful.
	- **Acts that can be reviewed:**
		- Legislative acts
		- Acts of Council, Commission, ECB, EP, or European Council
		- Acts of agencies/bodies intended to have legal effects
		- Excludes recommendations and opinions
	- Elements of an Action (4-step test)
		- Existence of a reviewable act
			- Any binding act producing legal effects 
		- **Legal standing** of the applicant
			- who can sue 
		- Grounds for review 
			- Why act is illegal 
		- Time limit 
			- 2 months publications or notification
	- Who can bring an action (Legal Standing)
		- Privileged applicants 
			- MS, Parliament, council, commission 
		- Semi Privileged applicants 
			- Court of auditors ECB committee of regions 
		- non privileged Applicants 
			- individuals and companies 
	- Locus Standi for Non-Privileged Applicants (Art 263(4))
		- A private applicant must fit one of these:
			1. Act **addressed directly** to them.
			2. Act **addressed to someone else** but of **direct and individual concern** to them.
			3. **Regulatory act** that directly affects them **without implementing measures**.
