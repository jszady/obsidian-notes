## Key Points

- What is supremacy of the EU Law ?
	- if there is a conflict between the provision of EU law and the provision of national law of a member state EU law will surpass it
	- [[Van Gen en Loos v Nederlandse Administratie de Belastigen]]
	- [[Costa v Ente Nazionale Per l'Energia Elettrica]]
		- look at slides for the points
- Who Applies the Supremacy of the EU Law ?
	- [[Amministrazione delle finanze delle Stato v Simmenthal SPA]]
		- Every national court must apply community law in its entirety
- What is the extent of the principle of Supremacy 
	- EU law takes primacy over constitutional law 
		- [[Internationale Handelsgesellschaft v Einfuhr]]
	- National Courts reaction to Supremacy of EU law
		- get from slides
		- [[Commission v Poland (15 July 2021)]]
		- 
- What is Direct Effect ?
	- this enables individuals to invoke and EU provision before a national or EU court
	- Individuals may take advantages of rights conferred by EU law and directly invoke EU acts before national and EU courts
	- [[Van Gen en Loos v Nederlandse Administratie de Belastigen]]
- Types of Direct Effect 
	- Vertical 
		- Individuals can enforce and EU provision against and "emanation of the state" (public body)
	- Horizontal
		- Individuals can invoke and EU provisions against another individual 
- What measures may have direct effect 
	- Treaty articles 
	- Regulations
	- Decisions
- Conditions for the Direct Effect of Treaty Articles and Regulations
	- The provision must be 
		- Sufficiently clear 
			- Everybody can understand what the provision is about
		- Precise 
			- the provision confers a specific right 
		- Unconditional 
			- the provision does not impose and condition upon the Member state for the citizens to be able to enjoy the right 
- Conditions for the Direct Effect of Directives 
	- Must be precise and unconditional 
		- [[Van Duyn v Home Office [1974] ECR 1337]]
	- The deadline for transposition into national law must be passed 
		- [[Pubblico Ministero v Ratti ]]
	- The defendant must be an emanation of the state
		- [[Marshall v Southampton Health Authority ]]
		- [[Foster v British Gas plc]]
		- the body has been made responsible by the state to provide a public service 
		- [[Farrell v Whitty and Others ]]
- Conditions for the Direct Effect of Decisions 
	- The provision must be sufficiently precise and unconditional 
	- the defendant must be addressee of the decision
